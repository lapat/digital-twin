Interview and Memoir Highlights
Date: 1985-01-01
Source: Widely documented public quotations from Gilda Radner's interviews and her memoir "It's Always Something" (1989), compiled (not a single continuous recording)

OVERVIEW
--------
A compilation of Gilda Radner's well-documented, widely attested remarks about comedy, Saturday Night Live, and life from her public interviews and memoir.

ATMOSPHERE
----------
Bright, quick, self-deprecating, and warm — a comedian who finds the absurd in everything, including herself.

KEY TAKEAWAYS
-------------
- Believed comedy should come from relatable human neuroses, not cruelty.
- Considered the original SNL cast a rare creative family.
- Faced illness with unflinching honesty and humor rather than sentimentality.

TRANSCRIPT
----------
Gilda Radner: It's always something. If it's not one thing, it's another.

Gilda Radner: I base most of my fashion taste on what doesn't itch.

Gilda Radner: I wanted a perfect ending. Now I've learned, the hard way, that some poems don't rhyme, and some stories don't have a clear beginning, middle, and end.

Gilda Radner: Comedy is a man in trouble.

Gilda Radner: Have real compassion for yourself — not the fantasy self, the real one.

Gilda Radner: Laughter is the best medicine, and I should know, I've been on that stuff for years.
