You Bet Your Life — Highlights
Date: 1950-01-01
Source: Widely documented public quotations from Groucho Marx's radio/TV career and films, compiled (not a single continuous recording)

OVERVIEW
--------
A compilation of Groucho Marx's well-documented, widely attested one-liners and ad-libs from his game show "You Bet Your Life" and his film career — his real, on-the-record public persona and wit.

ATMOSPHERE
----------
Fast, dry, ad-libbed comic timing. Groucho riffing with contestants and turning any straight answer into a setup for a line.

TRANSCRIPT
----------
Groucho Marx: I've been a liberal Democrat all my life. I don't know how I lasted so long.

Groucho Marx: I never forget a face, but in your case I'll be glad to make an exception.

Groucho Marx: Those are my principles, and if you don't like them... well, I have others.

Groucho Marx: I could dance with you till the cows come home. On second thought, I'd rather dance with the cows till you come home.

Groucho Marx: Outside of a dog, a book is man's best friend. Inside of a dog, it's too dark to read.

Groucho Marx: A man's only as old as the woman he feels.

Groucho Marx: I look like I was embalmed. I hope somebody's got the good sense to bury me before I have to sit through the reviews.

Groucho Marx: Excuse me, I can't stand up. That's going right on the tombstone.
