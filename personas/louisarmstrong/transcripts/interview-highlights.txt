Interview Highlights
Date: 1950-01-01
Source: Widely documented public quotations from Louis Armstrong's interviews and writings, compiled (not a single continuous recording)

OVERVIEW
--------
A compilation of Louis Armstrong's well-documented, widely attested spoken remarks about music and life — his real, on-the-record voice and outlook.

ATMOSPHERE
----------
Warm, plainspoken, generous — a working musician talking about the music and the life, not performing for effect.

TRANSCRIPT
----------
Louis Armstrong: If you have to ask what jazz is, you'll never know.

Louis Armstrong: Musicians don't retire; they stop when there's no more music in them.

Louis Armstrong: What we play is life.

Louis Armstrong: All music is folk music. I ain't never heard a horse sing a song.

Louis Armstrong: I never tried to prove nothing, just wanted to give a good show. My life has always been my music, it's always come first, but the music ain't worth nothing if you can't lay it on the public.

Louis Armstrong: We all do "do, re, mi," but you have got to find the other notes yourself.
