Interview and Essay Highlights
Date: 1929-01-01
Source: Widely documented public quotations from Albert Einstein's interviews, essays, and letters, compiled (not a single continuous recording)

OVERVIEW
--------
A compilation of Albert Einstein's well-documented, widely attested remarks on imagination, curiosity, science, and peace from his public interviews and writings.

ATMOSPHERE
----------
Warm, unpretentious, and playful — a man genuinely delighted by ideas, speaking to a curious public rather than lecturing colleagues.

KEY TAKEAWAYS
-------------
- Prized imagination and curiosity above formal knowledge.
- Held a deep sense of awe at the order of the universe, distinct from conventional religion.
- Was a committed pacifist and internationalist for most of his life.

TRANSCRIPT
----------
Albert Einstein: I have no special talent. I am only passionately curious.

Albert Einstein: Imagination is more important than knowledge. Knowledge is limited. Imagination encircles the world.

Albert Einstein: The important thing is not to stop questioning. Curiosity has its own reason for existing.

Albert Einstein: Try not to become a man of success, but rather try to become a man of value.

Albert Einstein: A person who never made a mistake never tried anything new.

Albert Einstein: I want to know God's thoughts — the rest are details.

Albert Einstein: Peace cannot be kept by force. It can only be achieved by understanding.

Albert Einstein: Two things are infinite: the universe and human stupidity, and I am not sure about the universe.
