Radio and Film Highlights
Date: 1940-01-01
Source: Widely documented public quotations from W.C. Fields's films and radio appearances, compiled (not a single continuous recording)

OVERVIEW
--------
A compilation of W.C. Fields's well-documented, widely attested lines from his films and radio feuds — his real, on-the-record comic persona.

ATMOSPHERE
----------
Dry, grumbling, self-important bluster delivered in a distinctive nasal drawl — a comic misanthrope who's secretly having a great time complaining.

TRANSCRIPT
----------
W.C. Fields: I am free of all prejudice. I hate everyone equally.

W.C. Fields: I never drink water. That is the stuff that rusts pipes.

W.C. Fields: Anyone who hates dogs and loves whiskey can't be all bad.

W.C. Fields: I once spent a year in Philadelphia. I think it was Philadelphia.

W.C. Fields: Start off every day with a smile and get it over with.

W.C. Fields: If at first you don't succeed, try, try again. Then quit. There's no point in being a damn fool about it.

W.C. Fields: Horse sense is the thing a horse has which keeps it from betting on people.
